Latest Nepal Income Tax Slabs (FY 2083/84)

The Government of Nepal introduced major reforms to the personal income tax system in the FY 2083/84 budget. For a detailed breakdown of all historical bands, you can view the complete Nepal Income Tax Slabs (2083/84) reference. The previous six-slab system has been simplified into five progressive slabs, the low-tax threshold has been doubled, and the highest personal income tax rate has been significantly reduced.

What's New in FY 2083/84?

The FY 2083/84 budget introduces one of the largest personal income tax reforms in recent years.

Key changes include:

  • Low-tax threshold increased from Rs. 5 lakh to Rs. 10 lakh.
  • Highest personal tax rate reduced from 39% to 29%.
  • Progressive tax structure simplified from six slabs to five.
  • Separate tax slabs for single and married taxpayers removed.
  • One common tax slab now applies to all resident individuals.
  • Lower monthly TDS for many salaried employees.

These reforms aim to increase disposable income while simplifying payroll calculations.

How Nepal Income Tax Is Calculated

Nepal follows a progressive income tax system.

This means:

  • Income is divided into tax slabs.
  • Each slab is taxed separately.
  • Moving into a higher slab does not increase tax on previous income.

For example, someone earning Rs. 18,00,000 does not pay 20% on the full amount. Instead, each portion of income is taxed at its applicable rate. If you are a business owner looking for step-by-step mathematical logic, refer to our comprehensive guide on How to Calculate Income Tax in Nepal.

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)

Employers deduct income tax every month through the Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) system.

Your monthly TDS depends on:

  • Annual salary
  • SSF contributions
  • Approved retirement contributions
  • Life insurance deductions (where applicable)
  • Applicable tax slabs

The calculator estimates your monthly TDS using the latest FY 2083/84 tax structure. For consultants or freelancers subject to different withholding rates, use our dedicated TDS Calculator Nepal to accurately determine your advance tax liabilities.

Social Security Fund (SSF)

Employees contributing to the Social Security Fund may qualify for certain tax benefits under Nepal's income tax provisions.

Where applicable, eligible SSF contributors may receive relief from the initial 1% Social Security Tax and other approved deductions.

Use the SSF option in the calculator to estimate your tax based on your contribution status.

Capital Gains Tax Changes

The FY 2083/84 budget also revised capital gains tax on listed shares.

Current rates:

  • Short-term holdings (up to one year): 10%
  • Long-term holdings (more than one year): 7.5%

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the Nepal income tax slabs for FY 2083/84?

The FY 2083/84 tax system uses five progressive slabs with rates ranging from 1% to 29%.

Is the first Rs. 10 lakh tax free?

No. Income up to Rs. 10 lakh is generally taxed at 1%, which is treated as Social Security Tax. Eligible SSF contributors may receive relief from this amount.

Are there separate tax slabs for married couples?

No. The FY 2083/84 budget introduced a single tax slab structure for all resident individuals.

How is monthly TDS calculated?

Monthly TDS is calculated by estimating annual taxable income, applying the progressive tax slabs, accounting for eligible deductions, and dividing the resulting annual tax across monthly payroll periods.

Does this calculator support SSF deductions?

Yes. The calculator estimates tax after considering applicable SSF-related adjustments where relevant.

Is this calculator based on the latest Nepal budget?

Yes. The calculator is designed to reflect the FY 2083/84 personal income tax structure. Always verify against the final enacted Finance Act and Inland Revenue Department guidance for official compliance.
