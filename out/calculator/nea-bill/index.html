The NEA Energy Tariff Principle

Electricity billing in Nepal operates on a highly progressive slab system that penalizes high consumption while subsidizing basic energy needs for low-income households (Lifeline tariff).

Total Bill = Fixed Demand Charge + Σ (Units in Slab × Slab Rate) + Penalty/Rebate

Fixed Demand Charge = Monthly fee based on Ampere capacity.

Units in Slab = Consumed units allocated to specific progressive pricing tiers.

Slab Rate = The cost per unit in that specific tier.

Penalty/Rebate = Time-sensitive financial adjustments based on payment date.

Understanding the Official NEA Tariff Rates in Nepal (2083/84)

The Nepal Electricity Authority (NEA) divides residential energy use into progressive slabs. This configuration ensures that minimal energy consumers pay lower baseline fees, while higher energy consumption draws higher rates. While looking back at historical data like the nepal electricity unit rate 2082 or the nepal electricity bill rate 2082 displays subtle baseline variances, the current fiscal cycle has fully transitioned to the updated nepal electricity bill rate 2083 framework mapped below.

This progressive structure applies directly to standard 5 Ampere, 15 Ampere, 30 Ampere, and 60 Ampere domestic connections across the country:

  • 0 to 20 Units Slab: Flat energy rate of Rs. 3.00 per unit (Note: Energy fee is Rs. 0 for dedicated 5 Ampere meters in this tier if consumption stays under 20 units, drawing only a base service charge of Rs. 30).
  • 21 to 30 Units Slab: Energy rate of Rs. 6.50 per unit.
  • 31 to 50 Units Slab: Energy rate of Rs. 8.00 per unit.
  • 51 to 150 Units Slab: Energy rate of Rs. 9.50 per unit.
  • 151 to 250 Units Slab: Energy rate of Rs. 9.50 per unit with increased corresponding service fees.
  • Above 251 Units Slab: Premium tier rate of Rs. 11.00 per unit.

How the Fixed Service Charge Works: You are not billed a cumulative sum of all service charges. Instead, your meter size (5A, 15A, 30 amp, or 60A) combined with your final consumption bracket determines a single flat service charge fee (ranging from Rs. 30 up to Rs. 250) applied at the end of the month. Knowing your exact electricity charges prevents surprise fees on your monthly statements.

⚠️ Crucial Lifeline Rule: If your 5 Ampere meter consumes even 1 unit over the 20-unit threshold, you are retroactively charged the base rate of Rs. 3.00 per unit for those first 20 units in addition to the progressive higher tier rates.

What is the new 5% VAT on electricity bills in Nepal?

Effective for the 2083/84 fiscal cycle, the Government of Nepal introduced a concessional 5% Value Added Tax (VAT) on domestic electricity consumption. This tax is not calculated on the gross bill total. The first 50 units consumed in any monthly billing cycle remain completely VAT-free. The 5% concessional VAT rate is exclusively applied to the portion of electricity consumption that exceeds 50 units.

What is the cost of 100 units of electricity in Nepal?

For a standard 5 Ampere residential connection, consuming 100 units of electricity costs approximately Rs. 1,040 under the current NEA tariff. This includes the cumulative energy charge of Rs. 965 (Rs. 365 for first 50 units + Rs. 475 for the next 50 units at Rs. 9.50/unit) plus a fixed service charge of Rs. 75. VAT of 5% applies on the energy charged above 50 units.

How much is a 150 unit electricity bill in Nepal?

A 5A meter consuming 150 units per month incurs an estimated bill of Rs. 1,440. The first 50 units cost Rs. 365 cumulatively, units 51–150 are charged at Rs. 9.50 each (Rs. 950), and a Rs. 75 fixed service charge is added. A 5% concessional VAT applies to the energy consumption above 50 units.

How much is a 200 unit electricity bill in Nepal?

For 200 units on a 5A meter, the estimated NEA electricity bill is approximately Rs. 1,940. Units 1–150 cost Rs. 1,315 cumulatively, and the remaining 50 units (151–200) at Rs. 9.50/unit add Rs. 475, plus a Rs. 100 fixed charge and applicable 5% VAT on usage above 50 units.

Progressive Slab Calculation: A Step-by-Step Practical Example

To demystify how the electricity bill calculator nepal methodology operates under the hood, let's examine a standard household consuming 150 units on a typical 5 Ampere meter using the official guidelines:

  1. Base Units Allocation: For any consumption crossing above 50 units, the cumulative baseline energy charge for your first 50 units is fixed at a combined total of Rs. 365.00 (calculated sequentially as 20 units @ Rs. 3, 10 units @ Rs. 6.50, and 20 units @ Rs. 8.00).
  2. Remaining Higher Tier Units: The remaining 100 units (from 51 to 150) are computed directly at the targeted slab energy charge rate:
    100 units x Rs. 9.50 = Rs. 950.00
  3. Total Energy Charge Subtotal:
    Rs. 365.00 + Rs. 950.00 = Rs. 1,315.00
  4. Fixed Service Charge Addition: Rs. 75.00 (Standard 5A allocation fee for the 51–150 units tier consumption level)
  5. Final Base Fuel Bill Estimate: Your actual statement dynamically aggregates the base progressive tiers, applicable late fine schedules, or early payment rebates. Running this scenario through our active calculator engine outputs a clean subtotal benchmark of Rs. 1,480.

Please note that your actual physical statement on the official nea billing portal may display small community energy duty adjustments, variations based on your safety margin meter capacity choices, or past due interest additions depending on your local distribution hub parameters.
