Every salaried employee and business owner in Nepal pays income tax — but most people do not fully understand how their tax is calculated, which deductions they are entitled to, or how the SSF enrollment changes their tax bill. This guide explains Nepal's income tax system for FY 2082/83 (2025/26 AD) in plain language, with full slab tables, worked examples, and answers to the most common questions we see from Nepali taxpayers.

## What is income tax in Nepal and who collects it?
Income tax in Nepal is a direct tax levied on the income of individuals, businesses, and organisations. It is governed by the Income Tax Act 2058 (2002 AD) and is collected by the Inland Revenue Department (IRD) of Nepal under the Ministry of Finance. The IRD operates through a network of Inland Revenue Offices (IROs) across the country, as well as an online portal at ird.gov.np.

For employed individuals, income tax is collected through a TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) mechanism — called PAYE (Pay As You Earn). Your employer calculates your estimated annual tax, divides it by 12, and deducts that amount from your monthly salary before paying you. The employer deposits this deducted tax with the IRD by the 25th of the following month.

Self-employed individuals, business owners, and freelancers must calculate their own tax liability and pay it quarterly (advance tax) or by filing an annual income tax return (ITR) with the IRD.

## Nepal income tax slabs 2082/83 — unchanged from 2081/82
Nepal uses a progressive tax system: higher slabs are taxed at higher rates, but only the portion of income that falls within each band is taxed at that band's rate. You do not pay 30% on your entire income just because you earn above Rs 10 lakh. You pay 30% only on the portion above Rs 10 lakh (for single filers).

The Finance Act 2082, passed by the Federal Parliament and gazetted in Ashadh 2082, confirmed that the income tax slabs for FY 2082/83 remain the same as FY 2081/82. There was no revision to slab thresholds or rates for the new fiscal year.

## The SSF 1% waiver — Nepal's biggest salary tax benefit
This is the most important tax rule for salaried employees in Nepal, and one that many people do not fully understand. Let us explain it clearly.

The first band of Nepal's income tax schedule is labelled '1%' and applies to the first Rs 5,00,000 of income for single filers (Rs 6,00,000 for married couples). This 1% is officially called the Social Security Tax (SST) — it is not regular income tax. It is collected under the Social Security Act 2074.

**The key rule:** If you are enrolled in the Social Security Fund (SSF) and making monthly contributions, this 1% SST is fully waived. You pay Rs 0 on Band 1.

For a single earner with Rs 5 lakh annual income, this waiver saves Rs 5,000 per year. For a married couple with Rs 6 lakh assessable income, it saves Rs 6,000 per year. For higher earners, the Band 1 waiver is still Rs 5,000 saved — which is meaningful, though a smaller proportion of total tax.

SSF enrollment is mandatory for formal-sector employers under the Social Security Act 2074 and Labour Act 2074. If your employer has not enrolled you, raise it with your HR department — non-enrollment is a legal compliance issue for the employer, and it is costing you Rs 5,000–6,000 per year in tax you should not be paying.

## Deductions that reduce your taxable income
Before applying the slab rates, Nepal's Income Tax Act 2058 allows several deductions that reduce your assessable income. These deductions are applied to your gross annual income (including festival bonus and all taxable allowances) to arrive at the assessable income on which tax is calculated.

| Deduction | Maximum Annual Limit | Key Rule |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| SSF employee contribution (11% of basic) | Whichever is lower: Rs 5,00,000 or 1/3 of assessable income | Also waives the 1% SST on Band 1 |
| EPF employee contribution (10% of basic) | Same Rs 5,00,000 / 1/3 cap (shared with SSF) | For non-SSF companies |
| CIT (Citizen Investment Trust) | Within the shared Rs 5,00,000 / 1/3 cap | Voluntary; combined with SSF/EPF |
| Life insurance premium | Rs 40,000 per year | Policy must be in taxpayer's name |
| Medical / health insurance premium | Rs 20,000 per year | Health insurance policy required |
| Principal residence building insurance | Rs 5,000 per year | Own-occupied residential building only |
| Remote area allowance | Up to Rs 50,000 | IRD-gazetted remote postings only |

The combined cap for SSF/EPF/CIT is the lower of Rs 5,00,000 or one-third of your assessable income. For most salaried employees earning Rs 5–15 lakh per year, the 1/3 cap is rarely hit — the Rs 5,00,000 absolute cap is the binding limit.

## The female 10% tax rebate — who qualifies?
Female taxpayers in Nepal whose entire income is from employment (salaries, allowances, and benefits from a single employer) are entitled to a 10% rebate on their total computed tax. This rebate reduces final tax payable — it is not a deduction from income.

The rebate applies after calculating tax on assessable income using the standard slabs. So if a female salaried employee's computed tax is Rs 50,000, her final tax is Rs 50,000 − Rs 5,000 = Rs 45,000.

**Who does NOT qualify:** Female taxpayers with business income, investment income, rental income, or income from more than one employer do not qualify for this rebate. The exemption is specifically for employees with a single employer and employment income only.

**Authority:** This rebate is provided under the Finance Act 2082, First Schedule, Note 2, read with the Income Tax Act 2058.

## How TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) works for employees
Employers in Nepal must deduct TDS from employee salaries each month and deposit it with the IRD. The process works as follows:

1. At the start of the fiscal year (Shrawan), the employer estimates the employee's annual income including festival bonus and all allowances.
2. The employer calculates estimated annual tax on that income after deductions (SSF, insurance, etc.).
3. This annual tax is divided by 12 to get the monthly TDS amount.
4. Each month, the TDS amount is deducted from the employee's gross salary. The remainder is the net salary deposited to the bank account.
5. The employer deposits the collected TDS to the IRD by the 25th of the following month via bank payment.
6. At the end of the year, if actual income or deductions differ from estimates, the employer adjusts the final months' TDS to ensure the correct total is deducted.

Employees receive a TDS certificate (Form 50 / Annex 5) from their employer at year-end. This certificate is used for filing ITR if required, or for claiming TDS refunds if excess was deducted.

## Frequently asked questions about Nepal income tax 2082/83

**Q: Is the income tax slab the same for 2082/83 as 2081/82?**
Yes. The Finance Act 2082 confirmed that income tax slabs for FY 2082/83 are identical to FY 2081/82. No threshold was changed and no new rate was introduced for employment income.

**Q: What is the 1% social security tax in Nepal — and is it income tax?**
The 1% on Band 1 is the Social Security Tax (SST) under the Social Security Act 2074, collected alongside income tax but legally distinct from it. For SSF-enrolled employees, it is zero. For others, it is Rs 5,000 maximum per year (single) or Rs 6,000 (married).

**Q: Where do I file income tax in Nepal?**
Through the Inland Revenue Department's e-filing portal at ird.gov.np, or at your nearest IRD Inland Revenue Office. The IRD has offices in all provinces.

**Q: Can I claim a TDS refund?**
Yes. If your employer over-deducted TDS (for example, if you had deductions or a rebate that the employer did not account for), you can file an ITR and claim a refund from the IRD. Refunds are processed by the Tax Office corresponding to your registration.

**Q: What is PAN number in Nepal, and do I need it?**
PAN (Permanent Account Number) is Nepal's taxpayer identification number issued by the IRD. All employees must have a PAN. It is required for bank accounts, property transactions, vehicle purchase, company registration, and many government services. Register at ird.gov.np.
