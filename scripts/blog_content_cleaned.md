# Nepal Freelancer Tax Guide FY 2082/83: The 5% Rule Explained

If you are a software developer billing a US startup, a graphic designer taking Upwork projects, or a YouTuber earning AdSense in USD — the Finance Act 2082 introduced one rule that simplifies your entire tax life: a **5% final withholding tax on income from IT and digital services exported abroad**.

For most freelancers earning under NPR 40 Lakhs a year, that 5% is it. The bank takes it, the IRD records it, and you are done.

Here is exactly how it works, who qualifies, and what you actually need to do before Ashadh ends.

## What Changed in FY 2082/83 {#what-changed}

Before FY 2082/83, earning from foreign clients meant navigating the same progressive tax slabs used for salaried employees — rates of 10%, 20%, 30%, and up. The filing process was complicated enough that most freelancers either ignored it or got it wrong.

Finance Minister Bishnu Prasad Paudel's FY 2082/83 budget fixed this. **Individual Nepali residents exporting IT and digital services in convertible foreign currency** now pay a single flat **final withholding tax** of 5% — deducted automatically at the bank, before the money even reaches your account.

## Who Qualifies for the 5% Rate {#who-qualifies}

You qualify if you are a Nepali individual resident in Nepal, providing services to foreign clients, and receiving payment in **convertible foreign currency (USD, EUR, GBP, etc.)** into a formal Nepal-based bank account. Eligible services include:

- Software development and programming
- Web and app development
- Data processing and cybersecurity
- Design and creative services (Upwork, Fiverr, direct clients)
- IT consultancy
- Cloud computing services
- Audio-visual content on YouTube, TikTok, Facebook, etc. — including **AdSense, brand deals, and affiliate income**
- Any digital or technology service delivered remotely to an overseas client

**Formal channels only.** Payment must arrive via SWIFT wire transfer, or authorized platforms such as Wise or Payoneer routed into a Nepali bank account. Informal channels — including Hundi — are **illegal** and disqualify you from the 5% rate entirely.

> **The Payoneer Card Mistake (This Comes Up Every Week)**
>
> I see this question constantly in the Entrepreneurs for Nepal Facebook group: "My Payoneer card works fine — do I still need to send money to my bank?"
>
> **The answer is no — the card does not count.** If the USD never physically arrives in a Nepali bank account linked to your PAN, you are not paying the 5%, you are not building a legal tax history, and that income will not appear on your Tax Clearance Certificate when you apply for a visa or home loan. Withdraw from Payoneer to your Nepali bank account. Every time. Without exception.

> **Remote Employee vs. Service Provider — Know Your Contract**
>
> If you are on a formal **payroll** of a foreign company (W-2 equivalent, fixed monthly salary, employment benefits), standard progressive slab TDS typically applies — not the 5% rule. If you work under a **service contract or consulting agreement**, the 5% rule applies. The label on your contract matters. If you are unsure, ask a CA before the fiscal year ends.

## How the 5% Tax Works — Real Examples {#how-it-works}

When foreign currency arrives at your Nepal bank, the bank deducts **5% as Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)** automatically. You receive 95%. That deduction is your complete income tax obligation on that payment — no further calculation, no separate filing for that income stream.

**Example 1 — Software Developer at a US Startup**

Pratik is a backend developer based in Kathmandu, billing a San Francisco startup USD 2,000/month on a service contract.

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Monthly gross (USD 2,000 × NPR 135) | NPR 2,70,000 |
| Bank deducts 5% TDS | − NPR 13,500 |
| Pratik receives | NPR 2,56,500 |
| **Annual tax paid (12 months)** | **NPR 1,62,000** |

Pratik earns NPR 32.4 Lakhs annually in foreign income — well under the NPR 40 Lakh threshold. His 5% TDS is final. No further income tax on this stream.

---

**Example 2 — Graphic Designer on Upwork**

Sarina is a UI/UX designer in Pokhara, earning an average of USD 800/month from international Upwork clients. Her payments go through Payoneer → Nepali bank account.

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Monthly gross (USD 800 × NPR 135) | NPR 1,08,000 |
| Bank deducts 5% TDS | − NPR 5,400 |
| Sarina receives | NPR 1,02,600 |
| **Annual tax paid (12 months)** | **NPR 64,800** |

> Check the live NRB exchange rate at [nrb.org.np](https://nrb.org.np) — the NPR 135 figure used here reflects the current NRB mid-rate range of NPR 134–136.

**The 5% is final** for incomes below NPR 40 Lakhs per year. If your total annual foreign IT income exceeds NPR 40 Lakhs, the standard progressive slabs apply to the **amount above the threshold only**.

## The No-Deductions Rule: A Trade-Off Worth Understanding {#no-deductions}

Here is the condition most guides skip. Under the **5% flat rate, you cannot deduct business expenses** — no laptop depreciation, no internet bills, no co-working space, no subscriptions. The tax is on your **gross amount received**, not net profit.

| Scenario | 5% Flat Rate | Progressive Slabs + Deductions |
|---|---|---|
| Income: NPR 20L, Expenses: NPR 2L | Tax = NPR 1,00,000 | Tax on NPR 18L net ≈ NPR 1,60,000 |
| Income: NPR 20L, Expenses: NPR 10L | Tax = NPR 1,00,000 | Tax on NPR 10L net ≈ NPR 50,000 |

For most freelancers with **low expenses**, the flat 5% wins easily. For those with **high deductible costs** — rented office, team equipment, significant software subscriptions — it is worth asking a CA to run the numbers. High earners above NPR 40 Lakhs can opt into the progressive system to claim deductions, but this removes the simplicity of the flat rate.

## Your PAN Must Be Linked — This Is Not Optional {#pan-linking}

The 5% TDS only gets credited to your IRD tax file if your **PAN number is linked to your bank account**. Without this, the bank still deducts 5% — but the money goes into the bank's general tax pool, not your personal IRD record.

**Consequences of an unlinked PAN:**

- IRD treats the income as undeclared during an audit
- You get charged back-tax at progressive slab rates (up to 39%) **plus 15% annual interest**
- Your Tax Clearance Certificate will not reflect this income

**How to link:** Visit your bank in person with your PAN card and citizenship document. Most branches complete this same-day. Verify the link through the IRD e-services portal at [ird.gov.np](https://ird.gov.np).

## Individual vs. Registered Company {#individual-vs-company}

As your income grows, your operating structure starts to matter. Here is how the two paths compare for FY 2082/83:

| Factor | Individual (PAN only) | Registered IT Company |
|---|---|---|
| Tax rate | 5% flat final tax on gross income | 20%–25% on net profit |
| Export benefit | 5% final withholding (under NPR 40L) | 75% tax exemption on export profits |
| Expense deductions | None under the 5% flat rate | Full business expenses deductible |
| VAT registration | Mandatory if turnover > NPR 20 Lakhs | Mandatory if turnover > NPR 20 Lakhs |
| Best suited for | Earnings below NPR 40L | Earnings above NPR 40L or hiring a team |
| Compliance complexity | Low — bank handles TDS automatically | Higher — bookkeeping, audit, annual filings |

The crossover point where a company structure typically becomes more tax-efficient is around NPR 40 Lakhs annual foreign income — the same as the flat-rate threshold. If you are approaching that level, talk to a CA before the fiscal year ends.

## The 75% Exemption: For IT Companies, Not Individuals {#company-exemption}

The FY 2082/83 budget also introduced a **75% income tax exemption** for registered IT service exporter companies (Private Limited, etc.) — separate from the individual 5% rule.

- Standard corporate tax rate = 25% (reduced to 20% for IT companies under this budget)
- 75% exemption on IT export profits effective rate of approximately **5% of net profit**

This applies to company profits — not your personal salary drawn from the company, which follows standard TDS rules.

**For startups:** Any startup with annual turnover up to NPR 10 crore is fully exempt from income tax for the first 5 years. Register properly, keep audited books, and verify eligibility with a registered tax practitioner.

## Standard Income Tax Slabs FY 2082/83 {#slabs}

For local salary income, domestic business income, or freelance income that does not qualify for the 5% rule, the standard progressive slabs apply. These are **unchanged from FY 2081/82**.

### Single / Unmarried

| Annual Taxable Income (NPR) | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to 5,00,000 | 1% Social Security Tax (SST) |
| 5,00,001 – 7,00,000 | 10% |
| 7,00,001 – 10,00,000 | 20% |
| 10,00,001 – 20,00,000 | 30% |
| 20,00,001 – 50,00,000 | 36% |
| Above 50,00,000 | 39% |

### Married (Joint Assessment)

| Annual Taxable Income (NPR) | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to 6,00,000 | 1% Social Security Tax (SST) |
| 6,00,001 – 8,00,000 | 10% |
| 8,00,001 – 11,00,000 | 20% |
| 11,00,001 – 20,00,000 | 30% |
| 20,00,001 – 50,00,000 | 36% |
| Above 50,00,000 | 39% |

*Source: Finance Act 2082, Inland Revenue Department Nepal (ird.gov.np).*

## SSF and the 1% SST Waiver {#ssf-waiver}

This is the detail almost every older tax guide misses — and it is worth real money.

If you contribute to the **Social Security Fund (SSF)**, the **1% Social Security Tax on your first income slab is completely waived**. That is a saving of NPR 5,000 per year if you are single (first NPR 5 Lakhs × 1%), or NPR 6,000 if you are filing jointly as a couple.

For FY 2082/83, the government is actively encouraging freelancers and self-employed workers to join the SSF. If you have any domestic taxable income alongside your foreign IT income, this is one of the simplest and most overlooked tax savings available to you right now. Check your eligibility at the SSF portal or ask your tax practitioner to set it up before year-end.

## Mixed Income: Foreign + Local {#mixed-income}

Many IT professionals in Nepal have a combination — a part-time local job plus freelance foreign income, or a local business alongside international clients. The two streams are taxed **separately and never combined**.

1. **Foreign IT income** 5% withheld at bank = final withholding tax, no further action needed
2. **Local/domestic income** Progressive slab rates apply, with TDS via employer or advance tax if self-employed

**Advance tax obligation:** If your domestic income tax liability exceeds NPR 7,500 for the year, you must pay advance tax quarterly.

| Deadline | Quarter |
|---|---|
| End of Poush | December/January |
| End of Chaitra | March/April |
| End of Ashadh | June/July |

Missing these deadlines attracts 15% annual interest. Use the [TDS Calculator on NepaCal](/calculator/nepal-tds-calculator/) to estimate your quarterly obligations.

## Step-by-Step Filing Process and Deadlines {#filing}

Even if your bank handles TDS automatically, staying compliant requires a few active steps each year. A clean tax record — and the **Tax Clearance Certificate** it produces — is essential for visa applications, bank loans, and government tenders. This is the part that matters when life happens.

**Step 1 — Register your PAN**
If you do not have a PAN, register at your nearest Inland Revenue Office (IRO). This is the foundation of your entire tax identity in Nepal.

**Step 2 — Link your PAN to your bank account**
Do this before any foreign payment arrives. Visit your bank branch with your PAN card and citizenship. It takes 15 minutes and is the single most important thing you can do for your tax compliance this year.

**Step 3 — Collect your TDS Certificate**

At the end of the fiscal year (end of Ashadh / mid-July), go to your bank and request a **TDS Certificate** confirming the 5% deducted in your name.

> **Pro Tip: Do not wait until the last day of Ashadh.**
>
> Some bank branches in Nepal — particularly smaller branches outside Kathmandu — are still confused about how the 5% IT export withholding rule works. Branch staff may tell you they cannot issue the certificate, or that the deduction was made under a different category.
>
> **What to do:** Bring a printed copy of the Finance Act 2082 notification or a screenshot of the IRD circular to show your branch manager. Ask specifically for the TDS certificate under the "IT service export withholding" category. If the branch cannot help, escalate to the bank's head office tax department. This is a known friction point — patience and documentation will get you through it.

**Step 4 — File your Annual Return (if required)**

| Situation | Action Required |
|---|---|
| Only foreign IT income under NPR 40L, no domestic income | No annual return required — bank TDS is a final settlement |
| Also have domestic income | File annual ITR (Form **D03** for individual income with foreign source) by end of Kartik (mid-November). Exclude the 5%-taxed foreign income from the progressive calculation |
| Foreign income exceeds NPR 40L | File return, pay additional tax on the amount above the threshold at standard progressive slab rates |

Note: The IRD Taxpayer Portal at [ird.gov.np](https://ird.gov.np) is your single point of truth for current form numbers. Use Form D03 for individual returns that include foreign-source income — confirm this at the portal before filing.

**Step 5 — VAT Registration (if applicable)**

If your annual turnover — **including foreign currency income** — exceeds **NPR 20 Lakhs**, VAT registration is mandatory for IT service providers. Once registered:

- You charge **0% VAT** on exported services (zero-rated) — no 13% added to foreign client invoices
- You still file periodic VAT returns with IRD
- You can claim **input VAT credit** on Nepal-based business purchases

The NPR 20 Lakh threshold applies specifically to service providers. A separate NPR 50 Lakh threshold applies to goods traders. Since your readers are IT freelancers, NPR 20 Lakhs is the correct and legally accurate figure to use.

## Deductions That Still Apply to Domestic Income {#deductions}

These reduce your progressive slab liability on domestic income only. None apply to income taxed under the 5% flat rate.

- **SSF/PF contribution:** Up to one-third of income or NPR 5,00,000 — whichever is lower
  > Eligible funds: EPF, CIT, SSF, and Pension Fund under the Pension Fund Act only. Other company provident funds were removed from the eligible list in FY 2082/83.
- **Life insurance premium:** Up to NPR 40,000 per year
- **Health insurance premium:** Up to NPR 20,000 per year
- **Approved donations:** Up to NPR 1,00,000 (100% deductible)

## Other Notable FY 2082/83 Changes {#other-changes}

**Digital payment VAT exemption:** VAT has been removed on digital payment clearing house services — reducing compliance costs if your business uses local payment platforms.

**IT companies as special industries:** Hotels and IT businesses now receive the same tax treatment as special industries, a category previously limited to manufacturing. This expands eligibility for certain concessions.

**Startup income tax exemption:** Startups with annual turnover under NPR 10 crore qualify for a 100% income tax exemption for the first 5 years. Maintain audited books and register properly with the relevant authority.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

**Is the 5% tax final for freelancers in Nepal?**

Yes — if your income is received in convertible foreign currency through a Nepal bank account linked to your PAN, and your total annual foreign IT income stays under **NPR 40 Lakhs**, the 5% deducted by your bank is a complete final settlement. You do not owe additional income tax on that amount.

**What are the income tax slabs for FY 2082/83?**

For single individuals, the first NPR 5,00,000 is taxed at 1% SST (waived if you contribute to the SSF), followed by 10% up to NPR 7L, 20% up to NPR 10L, 30% up to NPR 20L, 36% up to NPR 50L, and 39% above that. Married filers get a wider first slab of NPR 6,00,000 at 1%. These slabs are unchanged from FY 2081/82.

**Does the 5% tax apply if I get paid via Payoneer or Wise?**

Yes — but only if those platforms transfer the funds **into a Nepal-based bank account linked to your PAN**. Simply holding USD in your Payoneer balance or spending from a Payoneer card does not qualify. The money must physically land in a Nepali bank as convertible foreign currency for the 5% TDS to apply and register with IRD.

**Can I deduct my business expenses — laptop, internet, office rent — from the 5% taxable income?**

No. The 5% flat rate applies to your **gross income received** — no deductions are permitted. If you have significant business costs, ask a registered tax practitioner to compare whether the progressive slab system (which allows full deductions) produces a lower net tax bill at your specific income level.

**What happens if my freelance income exceeds NPR 40 Lakhs?**

You will need to file a regular annual return and pay tax at standard progressive slab rates on the **amount above NPR 40 Lakhs**. The portion under the threshold remains taxed at 5% final withholding. Consider whether a registered company structure becomes more efficient at this income level.

**Do I need to file an annual tax return if I only have foreign IT income?**

If your total foreign IT income is under NPR 40 Lakhs and you have no domestic income, you are generally **not required** to file an annual return — the bank's TDS is the final settlement. However, if you need a Tax Clearance Certificate (for a visa, loan, or tender), you should file regardless. The certificate requires a clean, updated IRD file.

**Does the 5% rule apply to IT companies (Pvt Ltd)?**

No. The 5% withholding tax is for **individuals only**. Registered IT companies receive a **75% income tax exemption** on profits from IT exports, bringing their effective corporate rate down to roughly 5% of net profit — a different mechanism with a similar outcome.

**When should I register a company instead of operating as an individual freelancer?**

The natural crossover is around NPR 40 Lakhs annual income — the same as the flat-rate threshold. Above this level, or if you plan to hire people, a registered IT company lets you deduct full business expenses and access the 75% export exemption. Talk to a CA before making the structural change, ideally before the start of a new fiscal year.

**What is the penalty for not linking my PAN to my bank account?**

IRD may treat unlinked foreign income as undeclared during an audit. Penalties include back-tax at progressive slab rates — up to 39% — plus 15% annual interest on the unpaid amount. PAN linking takes 15 minutes at your bank. There is no reason to delay this.
